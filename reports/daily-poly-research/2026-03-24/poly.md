### Stats, odds, and “how to bet” (framework)

This is **not** financial advice; it is a **decision checklist** for mapping Polymarket prices to position size.

1. **Separate implied from true probability.** Market price \(p\) is implied probability **if** the contract is efficient and rules are clear. Your edge is \(q - p\) (your subjective probability minus price).

2. **Binary Kelly (illustrative).** If you buy **Yes** at price **\(p\)** (for a **$1** payoff), one Kelly sizing rule is \(f^* = \frac{q - p}{1 - p}\) when your true win probability **\(q > p\)**. **Use half-Kelly or quarter-Kelly** in practice; prediction markets have fat tails and resolution risk.

3. **High-\(p\) markets (e.g. 85–95% Yes).** Expected value can be positive **only if** your \(q\) is even higher **and** you ignore **tail risk** (rule disputes, news shocks). **Risk of ruin** is real when you repeatedly “stack” near-certainties.

4. **Multi-bracket BTC.** The “modal” basket is not a hedge — adjacent brackets are **partial substitutes**. Consider **explicit** weights on **$68k–$70k** + **$72k–$74k** if you want to express **volatility** around the mode.

5. **Liquidity & fees.** Check **spread** and **depth** before sizing; taker-fee expansion **Mar 30, 2026** (see Platform News) affects high-turnover strategies.
