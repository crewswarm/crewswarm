## Dark Humor & Comedy Styles – Quick Research Findings

### 1. Core Characteristics of Dark Humor
- **Juxtaposition** – blends morbid or taboo subjects with light, witty delivery.  
- **Shock value** – uses surprise to provoke a laugh, then quickly undercuts it with absurdity.  
- **Self‑deprecation** – the writer often targets themselves, making the darkness feel safe.  
- **Economy of language** – short, punchy sentences keep the tone brisk and prevent gloom from lingering.

*Source*: “The Psychology of Dark Humor” – https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/laughing-mind/202303/the-psychology-dark-humor (snippet shows key traits).

### 2. Popular Dark Comedy Styles
| Style | Typical Tone | Example Techniques |
|-------|--------------|--------------------|
| **Deadpan** | Flat, unemotional | State a grim fact, pause, then add a trivial punchline. |
| **Gallows** | Morbid, ironic | Turn fatal outcomes into bureaucratic “processes.” |
| **Satirical** | Critical, exaggerated | Mock societal norms by pushing them to absurd extremes. |
| **Absurdist** | Surreal, chaotic | Combine unrelated dark images (e.g., “my coffee is as bitter as my soul”). |
| **Macabre Wit** | Elegant, literary | Use archaic diction (“lamentably”) to soften grim content. |

*Source*: “Types of Dark Comedy” – https://www.verywellmind.com/dark-humor-4177517.

### 3. Writing a Black‑Themed Comedic Bio – Best Practices
1. **Lead with a hook** – a startling fact about yourself, then undercut it.  
   *Example*: “I once tried to be a morning person. It didn’t work; I’m still allergic to sunrise.”  
2. **Mix personal quirks with universal taboos** – death, failure, existential dread.  
3. **Keep sentences ≤ 15 words** – maintains punch and readability.  
4. **Use concrete details** – a specific coffee brand, a favorite cursed movie, a recurring nightmare.  
5. **End on a relatable note** – a call‑to‑action that feels like an invitation, not a sales pitch.

*Source*: “How to Write a Darkly Funny Bio” – https://www.creativelive.com/blog/dark-humor-bio (key checklist).

### 4. Sample Phrases & Vocabulary
- **Adjectives**: grim, bleak, morbid, cursed, haunted, twisted.  
- **Nouns**: abyss, grave, morgue, fallout, black coffee, night‑shift.  
- **Verbs**: haunt, lurk, decay, misplace, stumble, cringe.  
- **Structure tip**: “I’m a [role] who [dark trait], but I also love [light, mundane thing].”

### 5. Competitive Landscape – Existing Dark‑Themed Bios
